How to Use This AR Experience

1Camera Permission

When prompted, allow access to your camera. This is required for the AR experience to work.

2Prepare the Marker

Hiro Marker Pattern

3View the Atom

Point your camera at the marker to see the hydrogen atom appear in augmented reality.

4Interact

Move around the marker to view the atom from different angles. Observe the electron orbiting the nucleus.

About the Hydrogen Atom

The hydrogen atom is the simplest and most abundant atom in the universe, consisting of:

  • 1 proton (in nucleus)
  • 0 neutrons (in nucleus)
  • 1 electron (orbiting nucleus)

Camera Access Required

To experience the hydrogen atom in augmented reality, we need access to your camera. This will allow us to detect the marker and display the 3D atom model.

No images or video will be stored - the camera feed is processed in real-time and only used for AR functionality.
