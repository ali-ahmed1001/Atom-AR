Hydrogen Atom Structure

The Simplest Atom

The hydrogen atom is the simplest and most abundant atom in the universe, consisting of just one proton and one electron.

Nucleus

At the center of the atom is the nucleus, which contains a single proton (shown in blue).

Electron Orbit

The electron (shown in red) orbits the nucleus at high speed along a specific path called an orbital.

Quantum Nature

In reality, electrons don't orbit like planets but exist in probability clouds. This visualization simplifies the concept for educational purposes.

How to Use This AR Experience

  • Allow camera access when prompted
  • Point your camera at a flat, well-lit surface
  • Look for the Hiro marker pattern on your second screen or print it out
  • Point your camera at the marker to see the hydrogen atom
  • Move around the atom to view it from different angles

Note: The electron orbits the nucleus every 4 seconds in this visualization.
